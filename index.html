Xavier Becerra
Democratic Party  |  Former U.S. Health & Human Services Secretary under Biden
👤 About
Becerra represented Los Angeles in Congress for more than two decades before being appointed California's attorney general in 2017, where he led the state's numerous lawsuits against the first Trump administration. He later served as U.S. Secretary of Health and Human Services under Biden, steering the administration through the COVID-19 vaccine rollout.
📋 Stands For
He's positioning himself as a "health care governor," open to revising the state's climate goals to keep fuel affordable for middle-class Californians, and wants to declare a state of emergency to freeze utility and insurance rates. He's also pushing toward a single-payer healthcare system.
⚠️ Controversy
Received criticism for the HHS agency's care of migrant children while he was Secretary. Opponents have attacked his record as a longtime government insider. Rival Tom Steyer has launched numerous ads criticizing his HHS tenure.
🤝 Endorsements
Planned Parenthood California, California Faculty Association, Equality California
💡 Bottom Line: One of the top polling Democrats and a fundraising leader. Seen as the establishment frontrunner but faces attacks from all sides.
Steve Hilton
Republican Party  |  Former Fox News Host / Former Adviser to UK Prime Minister David Cameron
👤 About
Hilton is British-American, and was senior adviser for UK Prime Minister David Cameron from 2010 to 2012 before moving to California, where he co-founded a political crowdfunding platform in Silicon Valley. From 2017 to 2023 he hosted a weekly show on Fox News.
📋 Stands For
He wants to lower gas prices by suspending environmental regulations, cut income taxes for middle-class and wealthier Californians, and open natural spaces for housing (particularly suburban single-family homes).
⚠️ Controversy
He stated he would extradite a California abortion doctor to Louisiana, a position that drew significant backlash. He has also been criticized for being an election denier, claiming Biden did not defeat Trump and calling for an investigation.
🤝 Endorsements
President Donald Trump, Nisei Farmers League, Israeli-American Civic Action Network
💡 Bottom Line: Trump's endorsed candidate and one of two Republicans in the top tier. His election denialism and abortion stance are major liabilities in a blue state.
Tom Steyer
Democratic Party  |  Billionaire Investor / Environmental Advocate
👤 About
Steyer is a billionaire who made his fortune at a hedge fund where he invested in such industries as private prisons and fossil fuels. After selling off those holdings, he founded a firm investing in environmental technology and became a climate change activist, sometimes bankrolling ballot measures on other liberal causes.
📋 Stands For
He's running on climate, economic justice, and protecting Medi-Cal from federal cuts. He supports the proposed one-time billionaire asset tax to backfill cuts to social programs.
⚠️ Controversy
His biggest liability is his wealth — rivals have repeatedly attacked him for trying to "buy" the governorship. Steyer has spent heavily on advertising to counter this narrative.
🤝 Endorsements
California Environmental Voters (co-endorsed with Porter)
💡 Bottom Line: Has spent over $200M on ads but polling has plateaued. His billionaire status is both his biggest asset and his biggest problem.
Chad Bianco
Republican Party  |  Riverside County Sheriff
👤 About
Bianco has a three-decade career in the Riverside County sheriff's office and was elected sheriff in 2018 with the support of the deputies union.
📋 Stands For
He wants to suspend numerous state regulations (particularly environmental ones). He states he will overturn the state's sanctuary laws which in general prevent the deportation of undocumented Californians. He also wants to boost oil and gas production, and eliminate the income tax and gas tax.
⚠️ Controversy
He has past ties to far-right groups such as the Oath Keepers militia and has faced criticism, lawsuits, and a state investigation over deaths and conditions in his jails. He also made national news when his office seized more than 600,000 ballots, which he described as normal law enforcement. Internal emails showed fringe groups had fueled that ballot seizure.
🤝 Endorsements
Peace Officers Research Association of California, California Republican Assembly, California Rifle and Pistol Association
💡 Bottom Line: The other top-polling Republican. He and Hilton are essentially competing to be the one Republican who makes it to November.
Katie Porter
Democratic Party  |  UC Irvine Law Professor / Former Congressmember
👤 About
Porter flipped a longtime Republican congressional seat in Orange County in 2018 and held it through 2024. She is a law professor focused on consumer protection, was mentored by Elizabeth Warren, and is known for grilling corporate and healthcare executives while pointing at a whiteboard.
📋 Stands For
She wants to cut income taxes for middle-income earners, raise corporate taxes on large businesses, and supports developing denser housing in urban areas and near transit stations.
⚠️ Controversy
Porter lost her 2024 Senate race to Adam Schiff, raising questions about her political momentum. She's also faced criticism from the left for not being progressive enough and from moderates for her sharp rhetorical style.
🤝 Endorsements
EMILYs List, California Environmental Voters, Teamsters California
💡 Bottom Line: Once considered a frontrunner but has slipped in polling. Still has strong name recognition and grassroots energy.
Matt Mahan
Democratic Party  |  Mayor of San Jose
👤 About
Born in San Francisco and raised in Watsonville, Mahan worked on voter engagement platforms in Silicon Valley before joining the San Jose city council in 2021 and becoming mayor in 2023.
📋 Stands For
He's focused on reducing street homelessness through tiny homes as an alternative to traditional shelters. A moderate, he opposes new taxes, wants to temporarily suspend the gas tax, and wants to tie government leaders' pay to performance metrics.
⚠️ Controversy
Critics say his shelter-bed approach isn't a sustainable solution and that it prioritizes temporary fixes over permanent affordable housing development.
🤝 Endorsements
Thrive LA (notably light endorsement list — newer face on the statewide scene)
💡 Bottom Line: The Silicon Valley moderate in the race. Strong betting market interest but struggling to break through in the polls.
Antonio Villaraigosa
Democratic Party  |  Former Mayor of Los Angeles / Former CA Assembly Speaker
👤 About
Villaraigosa was Speaker of the state Assembly in the late 1990s and mayor of Los Angeles from 2005 to 2013, during which he significantly expanded the size of the police force and pushed a local sales tax ballot measure to fund public transportation expansions.
📋 Stands For
He's running as a law-and-order Democrat with a focus on public safety, housing, and economic competitiveness. He's one of the more centrist Democrats in the field.
⚠️ Controversy
He's faced past scrutiny over an extramarital affair while mayor, and for his relationships with charter school interests.
🤝 Endorsements
Centrist Democratic business donors and some labor groups
💡 Bottom Line: An experienced but older name in California politics. Has struggled to stand out in a crowded field.

Josh Fryday
Democratic Party  |  Governor's Cabinet Member / Former Mayor of Novato
👤 About
Fryday heads Gov. Newsom's volunteer office since 2019. He previously served as mayor of the Bay Area suburb Novato, was the COO of NextGen America (Tom Steyer's climate org), and is a former Naval officer.
📋 Stands For
Expanding civic engagement, higher education access, and continuing Newsom-era priorities. Firmly in the Democratic establishment lane.
⚠️ Controversy
His close association with both Newsom and Steyer could be a liability. Critics note he's a relatively low-profile figure for a statewide race.
🤝 Endorsements
California Teachers Association, California Federation of Teachers, California School Employees Association
Janelle Kellman
Democratic Party  |  Environmental Attorney / Former Sausalito City Councilmember
👤 About
Kellman is an attorney and former Sausalito city councilmember who founded a climate nonprofit dedicated to studying sea-level rise. She earned her law degree from Stanford.
📋 Stands For
Climate policy, sea-level rise preparedness, and environmental law. The most environmentally focused candidate in the field.
⚠️ Controversy
Least well-known of the leading candidates with relatively thin statewide experience. Her fundraising has lagged behind the other frontrunners.
🤝 Endorsements
California Legislative Jewish Caucus (lighter endorsement list — she's the underdog)
Fiona Ma
Democratic Party  |  California State Treasurer
👤 About
Ma has been state treasurer since 2019, overseeing the state's finances as the world's fourth-largest economy. She previously served on the Board of Equalization, was a San Francisco supervisor, and served four terms in the state Legislature.
📋 Stands For
Leaning into her financial expertise — particularly around issuing housing bonds for universities and building student housing. She argues her deep experience in state finance makes her uniquely qualified.
⚠️ Controversy
Faced scrutiny during her time as treasurer, including questions about workplace culture in her office and management style.
🤝 Endorsements
California Labor Federation, State Building and Construction Trades Council, AFSCME
Gloria Romero
Republican Party  |  Former California Senate Majority Leader (formerly Democrat)
👤 About
Romero is a former longtime Democratic lawmaker who served nearly 12 years in the state Legislature, becoming Senate Majority Leader from 2005 to 2008 — the first woman to hold that role. She became a registered Republican in 2024.
📋 Stands For
School choice, rolling back COVID-era policies, and pushing back on Democratic gender identity policies in schools. Republican gubernatorial candidate Steve Hilton asked her to effectively run as his running mate on a joint ticket.
⚠️ Controversy
Many former Democratic colleagues and supporters feel betrayed by her party switch. Running in a heavily Democratic state as a new Republican makes a general election path very difficult.
🤝 Endorsements
California Republican Party, Reform California
Michael Tubbs
Democratic Party  |  Former Mayor of Stockton / Anti-Poverty Advocate
👤 About
Tubbs drew national attention when he became the youngest and first Black mayor of Stockton at age 26 in 2016. Raised by a single mother before earning a scholarship to Stanford. As mayor, he piloted a universal basic income program. He now leads nonprofits focused on ending poverty and expanding guaranteed income programs.
📋 Stands For
Universal basic income, anti-poverty policy, economic justice, and higher education access for low-income students. He's the most progressive candidate in the field.
⚠️ Controversy
His loss in Stockton raised questions about his electoral appeal beyond his base. Critics have also questioned whether his UBI advocacy is practical at a statewide scale.
🤝 Endorsements
Service Employees International Union (SEIU), one of California's most powerful labor organizations

Shirley N. Weber★ INCUMBENT
Democratic Party  |  California Secretary of State
👤 About
Weber, the daughter of Arkansas sharecroppers who fled the Jim Crow South, became the first Black Secretary of State in California history by appointment in 2021, after serving eight years in the Assembly. She then easily won a full four-year term in 2022.
📋 Stands For
Expanding voter access, defending universal mail-in voting, and prioritizing accuracy over speed in ballot counting. She's also focused on outreach to formerly incarcerated voters and younger voters.
⚠️ Controversy
Weber has faced ongoing criticism over California's notoriously slow ballot-counting pace, though she argues accuracy matters more than speed.
🤝 Endorsements
California Labor Federation and broad Democratic establishment support statewide
💡 Bottom Line: Heavy favorite. As the only Democrat and the incumbent, she is almost certain to advance to November.
Don Wagner
Republican Party  |  Orange County Supervisor / Former Mayor of Irvine
👤 About
Wagner is an Orange County supervisor, former mayor of Irvine, and ex-state assemblymember running on a platform of election integrity. He's the first major Republican to mount a serious challenge in this race in years.
📋 Stands For
He supports requiring voter ID at the polls, criticizes Weber for slow ballot-counting, and pushed for the release of sensitive voter information to Trump's DOJ over claims of voting irregularities. He also wants to streamline business filings and reduce fees for small businesses.
⚠️ Controversy
Many of his signature proposals — like ending universal mail ballots or imposing voter ID rules — would actually require new laws or ballot measures to implement, raising questions about what he could realistically accomplish. His push to hand voter data to federal investigators was particularly controversial.
🤝 Endorsements
California Republican Party, former Governor Pete Wilson as honorary campaign chair
💡 Bottom Line: Uphill battle in a blue state but the most credible Republican challenger this race has seen in years.
The Controller is California's chief accountant — overseeing spending, auditing state agencies, and paying government employees. Think of it as the state's top financial watchdog.
Malia Cohen★ INCUMBENT
Democratic Party  |  California State Controller
👤 About
Cohen was elected as Controller in November 2022, following her service on the California State Board of Equalization.
📋 Stands For
She's campaigning on governing state finances with transparency, efficiency, and fairness. She recently received the first clean audit opinion since 2019 — a win she's leaning into heavily on the campaign trail.
⚠️ Controversy
An undercover video surfaced showing a deputy controller official admitting the office "just can't conduct the audits" due to repeated staffing cuts, and acknowledging that agencies "none of them want us to go in there." Cohen's team noted that the office has faced years of budget cuts.
🤝 Endorsements
Broad Democratic establishment support; raised more than $1.2 million for re-election
💡 Bottom Line: Favored to win but her audit failures are a real vulnerability.
Herb Morgan
Republican Party  |  Chief Investment Officer / Finance Executive
👤 About
Morgan founded an investment firm in San Diego that was later acquired by Cantor Fitzgerald Investment Advisors. A finance industry executive with no prior elected office experience, running as an outsider candidate.
📋 Stands For
He's running on a fraud-focused scrutiny and tech-driven transparency platform, proposing tools like blockchain and AI for real-time spending oversight. He wants to aggressively investigate waste and fraud in California government.
⚠️ Controversy
Observers note some of his tech proposals may not be practical. He also has no government experience, which critics say is a major gap for an office that requires deep knowledge of public sector finance.
🤝 Endorsements
California Republican Party
💡 Bottom Line: Running as the finance expert outsider but faces a steep climb in a Democratic state.

Eleni Kounalakis
Democratic Party  |  Current California Lieutenant Governor
👤 About
With two terms as lieutenant governor under her belt, Kounalakis has plenty of experience on state boards. She regularly voted down proposed tuition hikes on the UC and CSU boards. She was previously ambassador to Hungary under President Obama. She originally ran for governor but dropped her bid late last year.
📋 Stands For
Fiscally responsible management of state investments, protecting working families, and keeping higher education costs down. She's pitching her board experience as uniquely relevant to the day-to-day duties of the treasurer role.
⚠️ Controversy
Her family's real estate development ties raised conflict-of-interest questions when she was lieutenant governor, as her office leases state space from properties connected to her family. Her late entry into the treasurer race after dropping out of the governor's race has also led some to question whether this is her true passion or a fallback position.
🤝 Endorsements
California Labor Federation, Teamsters California, National Union of Healthcare Workers
💡 Bottom Line: Leads in fundraising and name recognition. The frontrunner to advance to November.
Anna Caballero
Democratic Party  |  California State Senator
👤 About
A longtime politician from Salinas, Caballero served as the city's mayor, assemblymember, and state senator. She's developed a legislative reputation as a leader on agriculture and housing policy and was the state's top housing official under Gov. Jerry Brown.
📋 Stands For
She has vowed to simplify the application process for affordable housing subsidies and steer state resources away from federal immigration activities. Focused on practical fiscal management with particular strength on Central Valley and agricultural community concerns.
⚠️ Controversy
As a moderate, she has occasionally clashed with the Democratic Party's progressive wing on environmental regulation and criminal justice issues. Some have also questioned the depth of her financial expertise for a technically demanding role.
🤝 Endorsements
California Conference of Carpenters, California Latino Legislative Caucus, California Young Democrats
💡 Bottom Line: Strong challenger with deep Central Valley roots. Real shot at making it to November.
Tony Vazquez
Democratic Party  |  State Board of Equalization Member / Former Santa Monica Mayor
👤 About
A former councilmember from Santa Monica, where he served as the city's first Latino mayor, Vazquez now sits on the State Board of Equalization. In his campaign, Vazquez has stressed his Latino roots and his connection to storied California labor leader Dolores Huerta.
📋 Stands For
Economic justice, housing affordability financing, and expanding opportunities for working-class Californians. He's running as the most community-rooted of the three Democrats.
⚠️ Controversy
He's the lowest-profile of the three Democratic candidates and has significantly less fundraising than Kounalakis or Caballero. His main risk is being the spoiler — if he pulls enough Democratic votes, he could inadvertently knock one of the top two Democrats out of the November runoff.
🤝 Endorsements
Support from labor and Latino advocacy organizations
💡 Bottom Line: The wildcard. His vote share could determine whether this race stays Democrat vs. Democrat in November.
Jennifer Hawks
Republican Party  |  Retired Businesswoman / Silicon Valley Executive
👤 About
Hawks doesn't have experience in elected office. The longtime Silicon Valley retiree most recently served on the executive team at Sacred Heart Schools in Atherton. She is also a local Republican Party activist.
📋 Stands For
She argues her private sector background gives her the skills and independence to provide a genuine check on Democratic-dominated state government. She wants to bring a business-minded approach to managing state investments and debt.
⚠️ Controversy
Both her career background and her politics make her an outlier for a job that has been exclusively held by Democratic career politicians going on three decades. Her complete lack of government experience is her biggest hurdle.
🤝 Endorsements
California Republican Party, California Republican Assembly, Reform California
💡 Bottom Line: A long shot but the only Republican — if Democrats split the vote, she could slip into November.
The Attorney General is California's top law enforcement officer — executing the Governor's law enforcement directives, defending state laws in court, and leading lawsuits against the federal government. During both Trump terms, this has been one of the most active offices in the state.
Rob Bonta★ INCUMBENT
Democratic Party  |  California Attorney General
👤 About
Bonta was appointed attorney general in 2021 and was elected to a four-year term in 2022. Before that, he was an assemblymember representing parts of the East Bay. He is the first Filipino American to serve as California AG.
📋 Stands For
Since the reelection of Donald Trump, Bonta has filed or joined more than 50 lawsuits against his administration, making him one of the most active state-level fighters against federal policy in the country. He's also focused on consumer protection, fentanyl trafficking, gun enforcement, and reproductive rights.
⚠️ Controversy
In November 2025, Bonta acknowledged $468,000 from his reelection campaign went to a law firm for legal advice tied to an East Bay bribery investigation involving Oakland's former mayor and the Duong family. His wife, Mia Bonta, also sparked controversy by sponsoring legislation critics called a "journalism chill bill" designed to suppress stories about the family.
🤝 Endorsements
California Democratic Party, Governor Newsom, broad labor union support
💡 Bottom Line: Favored to win but the bribery investigation cloud is a real vulnerability heading into November.
Michael Gates
Republican Party  |  Former Trump DOJ Official / Former Huntington Beach City Attorney
👤 About
Gates is a former trial attorney and served as Huntington Beach city attorney for a decade, where he challenged lenient criminal justice policy from the state capital. In 2025, he was appointed a deputy assistant attorney general in Trump's DOJ Civil Rights Division, a job he left in November when he returned to the Huntington Beach city attorney's office.
📋 Stands For
Law-and-order enforcement, rolling back Sacramento overreach, restoring "election confidence," and cracking down on camping and loitering. He's running as part of a slate with Republican gubernatorial candidate Steve Hilton. He wants to refocus the AG's office on traditional crime-fighting rather than suing the federal government.
⚠️ Controversy
His challenges to state housing mandates and other Sacramento policies were frequently unsuccessful — supporters praise it as defending local control, critics see it as obstructionist. His brief stint in Trump's DOJ Civil Rights Division has also drawn scrutiny from civil rights advocates.
🤝 Endorsements
Steve Hilton, Huntington Beach Police Officers' Association, California Republican Party
💡 Bottom Line: The Republican challenger riding the Hilton-slate wave. Uphill battle in a blue state.

Ben Allen
Democratic Party  |  California State Senator
👤 About
A state senator whose district was directly hit by last year's LA fires. He's been deeply involved in insurance reform legislation at the state level and comes with strong environmental credentials.
📋 Stands For
Transparency from insurance companies, appointing a consumer advocate, revealing insurers' fossil fuel investments, banning commissioners from working in the insurance industry right after leaving office, and transitioning FAIR Plan customers back to the private market by building up fire resilience in high-risk communities.
⚠️ Controversy
Critics say his approach is heavy on environmental advocacy but lighter on concrete plans to actually bring insurers back to California. His focus on fossil fuel divestment is seen by some as tangential to the immediate coverage crisis.
🤝 Endorsements
California Environmental Voters, California Professional Firefighters, Sierra Club, U.S. Senator Alex Padilla, California Assembly Speaker Robert Rivas
Steven Bradford
Democratic Party  |  Former State Lawmaker / College Access Advocate
👤 About
A former state senator from the South Bay/South LA area with a long legislative career. He's pivoting from education advocacy to tackle the insurance crisis.
📋 Stands For
He would create a public-private partnership that shares risk with insurers to keep them in the state, link insurance requirements to land use and planning decisions, and support a voluntary buyout program to encourage people to move away from high-risk areas. The most pragmatic middle-ground Democrat in the race.
⚠️ Controversy
His pivot from education-focused advocacy to insurance regulation has raised questions about his depth of expertise. He received only about 9% of votes at the Democratic Party convention.
🤝 Endorsements
Teamsters California, State Building and Construction Trades Council of California, California Correctional Peace Officers Association
Jane Kim
Democratic Party  |  Former San Francisco Supervisor / Consumer Advocate
👤 About
A progressive former SF supervisor and consumer rights attorney with strong name recognition in the Bay Area.
📋 Stands For
She would establish "Natural Disaster Insurance for All," funded by a portion of policyholders' premiums, where customers stay with their insurers but a state-run fund would guarantee coverage for wildfire and flood damage. She would also create a public option for auto insurance.
⚠️ Controversy
Her proposals are the most expensive and ambitious, and critics question whether a state-backed insurance fund is financially viable given the scale of California's wildfire exposure. She's also seen as very SF-centric in a race that needs to speak to all of California.
🤝 Endorsements
U.S. Senator Bernie Sanders — a notable and unusual endorsement in a state-level insurance race
Stacy Korsgaden
Republican Party  |  Insurance Agent / Agency Owner
👤 About
Korsgaden has held an active California insurance license since 1988 and spent nearly 40 years running her own agency, winning national recognition for performance and customer service. She's the only candidate in the race with actual day-to-day insurance industry experience.
📋 Stands For
She wants to rebuild confidence in California's insurance environment by encouraging innovation and capital investment so insurers can offer a variety of products, restoring a strong and diverse insurance market. She's a free-market candidate who believes competition — not regulation — is the answer to bringing insurers back.
⚠️ Controversy
Critics argue that putting an insurance industry insider in charge of regulating the insurance industry is a conflict of interest. Democrats say the market-first approach is exactly what got California into this mess, since insurers were already free to leave — and they did.
🤝 Endorsements
California Republican Party, State Senate Minority Leader Brian Jones

The BOE oversees property tax assessment practices across California's 58 counties and hears tax appeals from businesses and individuals. District 1 covers inland California — including Sacramento. The race is down to 2 serious candidates.
Shannon Grove
Republican Party  |  California State Senator / Army Veteran
👤 About
Grove has represented the San Joaquin Valley in the Legislature since 2010, first in the Assembly and then in the state Senate, where she also served as Senate Minority Leader. She was the first woman veteran elected to the California Legislature. She's term-limited out of her Senate seat and pivoting to the BOE.
📋 Stands For
She's positioning herself as a taxpayer advocate with a proven record of stopping tax increases and fighting for Proposition 13 protections. She wants to bring conservative fiscal oversight to the board and push back on what she sees as Sacramento overreach on tax policy.
⚠️ Controversy
Her donors include AT&T, Phillips 66, Amazon, and the California Correctional Peace Officers Association — which progressive critics say shows she's beholden to corporate interests. Her record in the Legislature includes some votes that drew controversy on LGBTQ+ issues and social policy.
🤝 Endorsements
The Sacramento Bee endorsed her. Broad Republican establishment support. She has over $900,000 in her campaign account — a massive fundraising advantage.
💡 Bottom Line: The frontrunner with a huge money advantage. Likely headed to November.
Nelson Esparza
Democratic Party  |  Fresno City Councilmember / Educator
👤 About
Esparza is an educator who has served on the Fresno City Council since 2019, winning his 2022 re-election by 43 points. He has prioritized public safety, affordable housing, supporting working families, and equitable infrastructure investments. He previously served as a trustee on the Fresno County Board of Education.
📋 Stands For
Fair and equitable tax administration, protecting working families and small businesses from overtaxation, and ensuring the BOE serves all Californians — not just corporations with the resources to appeal tax assessments.
⚠️ Controversy
He faces a significant fundraising deficit — $203,143 raised compared to Grove's $863,704. The money gap is a real challenge in a race covering such a large geographic area.
🤝 Endorsements
California Democratic Party (endorsed with 90% of the vote at their convention), California Federation of Labor, Equality California
💡 Bottom Line: The Democratic contender with strong party backing but a steep fundraising hill to climb.

Doris Matsui★ INCUMBENT
Democratic Party  |  U.S. Representative since 2005
👤 About
Matsui was born in a Japanese internment camp during World War II. Elected in 2005 after her husband (who held this congressional district) died while in office. She's been in Congress for over two decades.
📋 Stands For
Clean energy, healthcare, technology policy, and flood control — she helped secure $4 billion in investments in local flood protections. Just this session she passed two bipartisan bills to strengthen emergency communication systems for wildfires and extreme weather. She pitches herself as someone who gets results through relationships and bipartisan deal-making.
⚠️ Controversy
At 81 she's one of the oldest members of Congress, and the generational change debate is very real. Her campaign has been accused by Vang of attempting to elevate a Republican candidate into November to avoid facing a stronger Democratic challenger. Between 2019 and 2024, she voted for five government spending packages that included nearly $42 billion for ICE, a record Vang has been hammering in a district with a large immigrant community.
🤝 Endorsements
California Democratic Party, Planned Parenthood, and broad Democratic establishment. The California Federation of Labor unusually endorsed both candidates in this race.
💡 Bottom Line: The established incumbent with deep roots, but facing her first serious challenge in 20 years. Redistricting has brought in new voters who don't have the same loyalty to her name.
Mai Vang
Democratic Party  |  Sacramento City Councilmember
👤 About
Vang is a Sacramento city councilmember and the first Asian-American woman and first person of Hmong descent elected to the Sacramento City Council. She is the first elected official to challenge Matsui in the 20 years since she took over her late husband's seat.
📋 Stands For
She's running on over 20 years of policy, governing, and community organizing experience, having secured millions for youth impacted by poverty and violence and led three successful citywide ballot measures. She supports Medicare-for-All, rejects corporate PAC money, and explicitly positions herself as the new generation vs. the old guard.
⚠️ Controversy
Despite her no-corporate-PAC pledge, the Matsui campaign pointed out she has taken tens of thousands of dollars from corporate PACs throughout her career (including from PG&E, casinos, and corporate developers) and has allegedly benefited from nearly $500,000 in super PAC spending.
🤝 Endorsements
National Union of Healthcare Workers, UAW Region 6, Working Families Party. The Sacramento Bee wrote that "for voters who want a new generation of Democrats in Washington with fire and conviction, Vang is that candidate."
